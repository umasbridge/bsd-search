- General Principles*
- In the 4th seat 2♦/2♥/2♠ are natural, 6+, 11-13; all vulnerability
- ns bid by resp = own suit (except systemic p/♣ bids), forcing to cheapest level of preempt suit.
  - Preemptor raises the suit with support/ Signs off without
  - If opponents interfere, x = support for p's ns; pass = no support
- All jumps by responder over a preempt are fit showing
- dbl of opps bid by preemptor's p = pen, except when systemically it asks for opener's suit
- 4n by p over any premeptive bid ( suit known) = rkc
- "Bid on" dbl = statement that " I think there is a save, what do you think?" eg: (1♥)1♠(2♠= lr)3♠(4♥)? now x by either = "bid on" ( only at first opportunity, else it is pen)

# Partnership Agreements

**Partnership notes**
1. In any gt, intermediate bid (last train) if available, = “not reject”, not necessarily “Accept”
2. Help suit gt: ns < 3M = no help, but values in ns; ns > 3M = help + values in ns; raise of help suit when hs = M: length + values in hs; when hs = m, raise = 2 honors (from AKQ); no length promised
3. 3 level (or higher, non jump) fs bid by PH = LR in opener’s 2nd suit, fs is artificial; same for 2 level when the bid is not a Gazilli/ xyz bid: Eg. p1♥1♠2♦3♣ = LR in ♦; p1♦1♠2♣2♥ = LR in ♣

---

**Agrements in competttion**
1. When they 'x' our lr bid; xx by us = accept game; eg 1m2m(x)xx = gf
2. In competition, we spl only in the enemy suit; except in the following cases
  - a cue raise, followed by a jump in ns = spl
  - when < 2 new suits available for bidding naturally, jumps in ns = spl. Eg: …
3. When opps have bid 1 suit then bidding/ raising that suit = Michaels (1x)2x. This also applies when they bid via a transfer: eg: (1n)p(2♦)?, x = ♦ suit, 2♥ = Michaels
4. If opps bid 2 suits, say (1♣)p(1♥)?, bidding responder’s suit (2♥)  = nat; opener’s suit (2♣) = Michaels. Same when the 2nd suit is bid via a xfer; eg (1♣)p(1♥ = 4+♠)? x = ♥; 1♠ = ♠ suit; 2♣ = Michaels
5. When opps interfere over our art bids (below game level), based on context: x /xx = forward going, bal/ stolen bid; pass = worry / unable to make normal bid; rest – as if no interference
  - Interference over our 1n/2n/2♦: opener to pass w/o stop if below committed level
  - 1♠3♣(3♥): x = ♥ sing;  pass = bal ( would have bid 3♦); rest same
  - 1♥1n2♣(x): xx = 8+
6. In competition, cue followed by ns/own suit/ support = gf

# Hand Ownership

The following rules apply
1. 2♣/2n opening – they cannot play game undoubled;
2. if both sides have shown values, the side that makes a cue(lr+) owns the hand
3. All gf situations, we own the hand
4. I open and p shows 10+ (via a lr bid / 2 level o/♣ / xx), they cannot play game undoubled only if they are outgunned in hcp, not when both are bidding on shape
5. One side is a PH facing a non game level pre-emptor – the other side owns the hand
6. We have shown values, and at least one of us did not get a chance to bid game because of their auction: 1♥(1♠)2♠(4♠) or 1♥(1♠)x(4♠), the hand belongs to us (only at game level)

# Forcing Pass

**Forcing**** pass (fp)**: pass = let p decide (later pull = offense + strength), dbl = regressive, bid = offense. Pass and pull at higher level shows control in enemy suit, while a direct cue does not.
1. When a rebid is promised and fit not found, forcing pass = min (planned to pass any nf rebid by p), a bid of one of our suits = extra length / support and ns bid = shape, not much hcp (with shape + hcp, pass then bid ns – pull) eg: 1♥(1♠)2♦(2♠)? here pass = no interest ( would have passed any nf rebid by p) 3♣ = shape (playability at 3 level if p corrects to 3♥); 3♦/ 3♥ = support / extra length

# Fit Jumps

In competition, Jumps are **fit showing (fsj)** (except if it has systemic meaning).
1. Fsj are forcing upto the cheapest bid of opener’s suit, except 1m(x)2M = nf
2. Fsj of a m, when p has bid M, promises values (tricks in the minor) and 3+M
3. When p has o/c, fsj  only when rho has bid. If rho passes: non jump = f1; jump = wk
4. Fit jumps do not establish hand ownership. Fit jumper’s P can establish ownership through a double or cue. If cue is available below game, then x = gt, else x = we own the hand
5. All 4ns bids (even if no jump by UPH) = support + cards in bid suit
6. Jumps at 4 level in competition after we bid/ overcall M: eg 1♥(x)4♦, 1♥(1♠)4♦, shows big fit and playing strength (typically 1 high card each in both suits) to enable 5 level decision
  - geenrally weakish, eg 1♥(3♣)? Jx xxxxx Axxx Ax, bid 4♦ (direct 4♥ denies any outside cards)
  - but (1♥)1♠(2♠)? 4♦ is also the right bid when there is no other bid to show values + raise - kxxx xx akjxx xx

# Leads

**Opening Leads**
1. 2/4 in nt. From 3 worthless cards (9xx or worse) lead top card, from Hxx, lowest
2. 3/5 th in suits and 4nt/5nt/6nt/7nt, 2/4 in 1/2/3 NT
3. Journalist leads in suit contracts only, and only on opening lead
4. Top of nothing/ low with an H from a known 3 card holding (may have supported partner)
5. A from AK in suit, except in high level contracts/ doubled contracts, K from AK( or Q from AKQ) may be played asking for count
6. K from AK in nt, here A asks for unblock/count
7. Lead of a Q in nt, asks for unblock of J  or attitude
8. Lead of a K (which holds) followed by a ns card, indicates that the ns card is a singleton

**When leading in the middle of the game**
1. When on lead in the middle of the game, lead from the current holding as if on opening lead (except in nt: when returning suit led by p, and if dealt 5 cards in the suit, lead original 4th best)
2. Attitude leads and shifts in the middle:
  - spot card  <7 = non -ve attitude (have H, or don’t want shift); led card = count card
  - spot card 7 or higher = -ve ( no H, don’t want the suit returned). Low card ( +ve attitude)

# Carding

1. Count and Present Count = udca
2. count on declarer’s lead (count need not be given if not relevant or already known – in the latter case, suit preference implications may be there)
3. Attitude on partner’s lead consistent with [obvious shift principle|popup|page-Obvious-Shift-Principle]
4. First discard is attitude – order of discards may indicate suit preference
5. Playing an honor on p’s lead promises the touching lower honor. If that info is not relevant (there is no logical reason for p to play an H) suit preference implications may be there
6. While splitting, play lower honor from 2 touching, and highest from 3 touching honor

# Obvious Shift Principle

1. Attitude signal at Trick 1:
  - Encourage = like led suit/ don’t want obvious shift/ have no preference (passive)
  - Discourage = want obvious shift
2. This applies even when dummy is a singleton or void, (we do not play suit preference here)
  - Enc ( low card) =  non obvious shift ( or could be continue suit to tap dummy’s trumps )
  - Disc (High card)  = obvious shift indicated

**Finding the obvious shift suit ( trumps can be the obvious shift)**
**First**, see if there a suit where we are in danger of losing tricks if we don’t cash (dec’s losers can be discarded et.) then that suit is the obvious shift
When the above is not obvious/ absent; use the following guidelines to identify Obvious shift
1. A suit bid by us:  if both have bid different suits, and p leads the 4th suit ( 3rd suit is trumps) then obvious shift = suit bid by partner (opening leader)
2. Dummy’s weak suit
3. Higher ranking of equal suits ( H to 3/ H to 4; H = A/K/Q)
4. Trumps if ruff is a threat
5. Dummy’s short suit in nt (when dec is known not to have length in that suit) – even with a strong holding like AK

The following are generally not obvious shift suits
1. A suit bid by opps
2. Dummy’s stiff / void (in a nt contract this may be obvious shift)
3. Dummy’s long suit

# Methods to consult partner on potential 5 level competition

1. **Cue asuggesting sarifice:** Lho opens, p overcalls, rho makes a value bid: (1♠)3♣(x)3♠; (1♠)2♣(3♣)3♠; Now 3 level cue or higher by advancer = suggest sacrifice at one level > their game level
2. **Fit Double Jump/ 4 level single jump**: Jumps at 4 level in competition after we bid/ overcall M: eg 1♥(x)4♦, 1♥(1♠)4♦, shows big fit and playing strength (typically 1 high card each in both suits) to enable 5 level decision
  - generally weakish, eg 1♥(3♣)? Jx xxxxx Axxx Ax, bid 4♦ (direct 4♥ denies any outside cards)
  - but (1♥)1♠(2♠)? 4♦ is also the right bid when there is no other bid to show values + raise - kxxx xx akjxx xx
3. After we have found fit, new suit bid at the 4 level, (or 3 level > 3 of trumps) = playing strength bid ( with strength, double). Eg 1♥(2♣)2♥(3♣)3♠ = values in ♠ (partner can look at ♠ to consider 5 level competition)
